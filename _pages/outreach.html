In my community

I would not be where I am today without mentors and scientists who guided and inspired me. I want to be that person for young students, showing them what the broad range of what a scientist can look like. Through programs like SciREN and SciMatch, I get to create learning experiences for students to engage with research and see what it's like to be a scientist just like me.

SciREN

SciREN (Scientific Research and Education Network) brings scientists across North Carolina to share curriculum-aligned lesson plans rooted in real research happening across the state with NC K-12 educators. I have been involved both as an organizer, helping plan and run the annual event, and as a participating scientist, sharing a lesson plan inspired by my own work.

Being on the organizing team means I get to see the full picture: from recruiting scientists to matching them with teachers who can bring that science into the classroom. It is rewarding work that reminds me why I became a researcher in the first place.

SciMatch

SciMatch is part of NC SciFest, a statewide science festival presented by RTI International. SciMatch pairs scientists with students for mentorship and hands-on engagement with the research process. I presented my work to 4 classes of middle school students to share what a career in science can look like for students who might not yet see themselves in that role.
